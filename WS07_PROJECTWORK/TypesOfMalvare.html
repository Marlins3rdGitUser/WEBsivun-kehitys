Types of Malware

After all, it is not as simple as it looks...

Malware

As mentioned in the Homepage, Malware is a type of software or a program that acts malicious in order achieve it's desired effect, whetever it be to steal information or encrypt files from a device, system or a network, to turn the device into a money maker (Usually a crypto miner) or just simply to cause damage to the device, system or the network the device is connected to.

Every piece of malware is made with a goal in mind, but often than not they are used to make money for the malwares creator (Who are commonly referred to as "hackers.""). Malwares are often designed to avoid detection from various different antivirus softwares so that it can accomplish it's goal. Some malware may tamper with the antivirus systems to exclude itself from possible scans the antivirus software may do.

Here is list of six types of common malware that can be found.

(There are more examples than these that are more dangerous, but these are most commonly known ones.)

Adware

Adware is the most common type of malware that basically spams the infected computer with advertisements. Very annoying but relatively harmless to the user.

Trojan Virus

Trojan virus is a type of malware that disguises itself as normal software or a program. When installed, it begins to collect information silently from the device and send it to the hacker.

Cryptojacker

Cryptojacker is type of malware that makes the computer use it's resources to run a cryptominer for the hackers profit. It is often silent and often runs in the background, making you computer use more power than normal.

Spyware

Spyware is a type of malware that allows the hacker to see the screen of the infected device. Often used to collect information about the user of said device.

RAT malware

RAT malware stands for "Remote Access Trojan" and it is a type of malware that allows the hackers to access the infected device and take full control of it.

Ransomware

Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts all files on a device or network and demands that the user(s) pay money to have the files be decrypted
